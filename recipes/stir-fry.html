Chicken Stir-Fry

Description

This chicken stir-fry recipe is a quick and versatile dish that anyone can make. It features juicy, tender chicken and flavorful vegetables. Serve it with rice or noodles for a satisfying meal.

Serves 6 people with a prep time of 20 minutes and a cook time of 20 minutes.

Recipe from: allrecipes

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups white rice
  • 2/3 cups soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli flakes
  • 3 skinless, boneless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons oil, divided
  • 1 head broccoli, broken into florets
  • 1 onion, cut up in large chunks
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut up

Steps

  1. Boil rice in water at high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer rice until fluffy, and water has been absorbed, for 20 to 25 minutes.
  2. While rice is boiling, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, and cornstarch in a bowl. Stir in ginger, garlic, and chili flakes. Add chicken and mix well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of oil to a skillet at medium-high heat, and add the vegetables. Cook and stir them until tender, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a dish and set aside.
  4. Add the remaining tablespoon of oil in the same skillet at medium-high heat, and add the chicken with some marinade. Cook the chicken until just browned, for 2 minutes per side. Stir in the vegetables and add the remaining marinade. Cook the chicken until it's not pink inside and vegetables are tender, for 5 to 7 minutes. Serve it over rice.
